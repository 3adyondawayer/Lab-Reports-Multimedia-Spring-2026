High-Pass Filter Design

High-pass filters are the complement of low-pass filters - they enhance high frequencies (edges, details) while attenuating low frequencies (smooth regions).

DC component removal: High-pass filters remove or greatly attenuate the DC component (average brightness), making the image appear dark. The DC component represents the overall illumination level of the image.

High-frequency emphasis: This technique combines the original image with its high-pass filtered version, preserving the overall appearance while enhancing edges. It's more practical than pure high-pass filtering for most applications.
